6.1 Introduction

Steam boiler is a closed vessel in which heat produced by the combustion of fuel is utilized to generate steam from water,at destioned temprature and pressure.Boiler is a device used for generating steam which is used for driving prime movers like steam turbines or industrial purpose like process heating.

According to Indian Boiler Regulation(I.B.R.)Act 2007.

"Boiler" is a closed pressure vessel in Which steam is generated with capacity exceding 25 litres,gauge pressure is greater than or equal to 1 kg/cm²,and water is heated at 100°C or above.It includes all the mounting fitted to such vessels which remain wholly or partly under pressure when steam is shut-off.

The boiler used for power generation has condiderably large capacity and steam is generated at high pressure to get high efficiency of turbine.The boiler used for process heating and steam is generated at a low pressure.It is generally smaller in size and simpler in design.

Classification of Boilers

The boiler can be classified as follows.

  1. Accordiing to geometric orientation of boiler
    2. If the axis of the boiler is horizontal,the boiler is known as horizontal boiler,if the axis is vertical,it is known as vertical boiler and if the axis is inclined it is known as inclined boiler.
    Example:Horizontal boilers:Lancashire boiler,Locomotive boiler.
    Vertical boilers:Cocharan boiler.
  2. According to relative position of water and hot gases
    1. Fire tube boilers ∶If the hot gases of combustion from the furnace pass through the tubes and water is surrouding the tubes is called fire tube boilers.
      Example:Cocharan,Lancashire,Locomotive.
    2. Water tube boiler ∶If the water passes through the tube and hot gases surrounding the tulbes is called water tube boiler.
      Example:Babcock and Wilcox boiler.
  3. According to location of furnace
    1. Extrenally fired boilers ∶In this boiler the furnace is placed outside the boiler shell.
      Generally water tube boilers are externally fired.
      Example: Lancashire,Cocharan boiler.
    2. Intrenally fired boilers ∶In this boiler the furnace is placed inside the boiler shell.
      Generally fire tube boilers are internally fired boiler.
      Example: Lancashire,Cocharan boiler.
  4. Accordiing to method of water circulation
    1. Natrally circulation boilers ∶In this,water flow take place naturally,by density difference of water.The flow of water and steam are set up due to density difference resulting from diffrence in temprature.The water from steam drum falls under gravity to header where its temprature rises and density reduces,steam moves upward to steam drum to low density.Normally,the low capacity boilers are natural circulation type boiler.
      Example:Lancashire,Babcock and Wilcox boiler.
    2. Forced circulation boiles ∶In this boiler,water flow(circulation) takes place by a pump.High pressure boilers are forced circulation boilers.
      Example:Benson boiler,La-mont boiler,Velox boiler.
  5. According to working pressure
    1. High pressure boiler ∶The working pressure of this boiler is higher than 25bar.
      Example:Babcock and Wilcox boiler
    2. Medium Pressure boiler ∶The range of working pressure of this boiler is between 10 bar to 25 bar.
      Example:Lanacashire boiler,Locomotive boiler.
    3. Low pressure boiler ∶The range of working pressure is between 3.5 to 10 bar.
      Example:Cocharan an Cornish boiler
  6. According to mobility of boiler
    1. Stationary boiler ∶This boiler cannot be moved easily from one place to another place.The stationary boilers are used for power generation or process heating in industries.
      Example:Lancashire,Babcock and wilcox boiler.
    2. Mobile boiler ∶It is portable boiler and can be easily moved from one place to another place to another place.This boiler is used in marine and locomotive
      Example:Locomotive boiler.
  7. According to numbers of tubes in the boiler
    1. Single tube boiler ∶This boiler having only one fire or water tube for circulation of hot gases or water.
      Example:Cornish boiler.
    2. Multi-tube boiler ∶This boiler havinf=g two or more fire or water tubes for the circulation of hot gases or water.

6.3 Advantages and Disadvantages of water tube boiler over the fire tube tube boiler

Advantages of water tube boiler:
  1. The heating surface of the boiler per unit space occupied is greater.
  2. The water tube boiler is required a smaller floor area compared to a fire tube boiler.
  3. The fuel is complerely burnt due to the large heating area of furnace.
  4. A good and fast circulation of water can be possible,and the temorature is maintaing uniform throughout the diffrent parts of the boiler.
  5. Steam can be raised more quickly therefore it can be used for variable load.
  6. The different parts of the boiler can be seperated , therefore easier to transport.
  7. Maximum amount of heat trasfer is possible due to hot gases travel approximately at right angle to the heating surfaces.
Disadvantages of water tube boiler:
  1. Scale deposition will cause overheating and bursting of tubes.
  2. Proper control system is required to maintain consstant level of water as there is small reserve of water.
  3. The maintanace cost is higher than that of fire tube boiler.

Essential requirements for a good boiler

A good boiler should have the following qualities.
  1. It should produce maximum steam per unit fuel consumption to increase the effeciency of boiler.
  2. Steam production rate should ben as per requiremnts.
  3. It should be light in weight and occupy minimum space.
  4. It should be as per IBR(Indian Boiler Regulation)
  5. It should be able to generate steam with variable rate as per requirements in wide range.
  6. The velocity of water and fire gases should be minimum to minimize frictional loses.
  7. Tubes should be sufficiently strong to resist wear and corrosion.
  8. Mud and other deposit on hearing surfaces.
  9. It should be easy to maintain and inspect.

6.10 Lancashire Boiler

Lancashire boiler is simple in design,easily to operate and less operating and maintanace coat.It is one of the most commonly used stationary boilers.It is normally used in sugar mills,textile industries where power generation at well as process heating is required.

Specification :Shell diameter 2 to 3 m,length of the shell 7 to 9 m,maximum working presure 16 bar,steam capacity 9000kg/hr,efficiency 50 to 70%

Characteristics :Horizantal,stationary,fire tube,internally fired multi-tube(two fire wire),natural circulation of hot gases,medium pressure boiler.

Construction :It consists of a cylindrical shell and two fire tubes as shown in fig.The cylindrical shell is placed over the brick structure.The boiler have three passes for flow of flue gases.One flue passes from inside of boiler and through fire tubes,is called main flue (MF).Second flue passes from below the shell is called bottom flue (BF)and third from the side of boiler is called side flue(SF).The side flue and bottom flue passes are formed by brick work.The fuel grates area provided at the front end and inside of two main fire tubes.A fire bridge is provided at the end of the grate to prevent coal and ash particles entering into the interior of the furnace tubes.Superheater is provided at the end of the main flue tubes in passage of flue gases.While an economiser is at the end of the side flues,before exhausting the gases to chimney.Dampers(sliding doors)are placed at the end of the side flues to control the flow of gases.

Working :The coal is introduced to the grate through fire holes.The combustion of coal takes places in pressure of air which is regulated by damper.The combustion will produce hot gases.

Path of flue gases :The hot gases from the grate pass upto back end of the tubes and then in the downward dirction(MF to BF).They move by the bottom flue to the front of the boiler where they are divided into two streams and pass into the side flues(BF to SF).They move along two side flues and enter the chimney and discharged to atmoshpere.

Path of flue gases as under.

Grate → Fire tube(MF) → BF → SF → Chimney → Atm

Due to this flue gases path,the water in the shell is heated from bottom by the bottom by the bottom flue,from sides by side flue and from centre by fire tubes.

Damper regulates mass flow rate of flue gases.Ultimately it regulate fuel combustion are as well as steam generation rate.Dampers are operatesd by chain passing over a pulley outside the boiler.

Mounting :The pressure gauge and water level indicator and feed check valve are provided at front of boiler.On the front side, the blow off cock is provided at the bottom.The fusible plugs are mounted on the top of the main flues just over the grate.The anipriming pipe,safety valve,low water and high steam safety valve and manhole are provided on the top of boiler shell.

Advantages :

  1. Due to three passes of flue gases,the heating surface area per unit vvolume of boiler is large.
  2. The fluctuations in load can be easily met by this boiler due to large reservoir.
  3. Easy operation,low maintenance costs,easy to clean and inspect.
  4. By use of economiser and superheater,maximum heat of flue gases is utilized,so efficiency of boiler can be increased.

Disadvantagess :

  1. Maximum working pressure is limited to 16 bars.
  2. Due to brick work,more floor area is required.
  3. Response of pressure build up is less.
  4. The furnace is inside the tubes therefore the grate area is restricted.

6.13 Boiler mountings

(1) Pressure Gauge

Function: It indicates the pressure of steam in boiler.

Construction: The gauge is usually mounted on the Front top of the shell or on the drum.It is having dial which graduated to read pressure in kg/cm² or bar,above atmoshere.A bourden tube gauge with its inteior mechanism is shown in Fig.The circular bent bourden tube of oval-cross section is closed at one end and connected at the other end to steam space of boiler through siplon(U-tube).The siphon is filled with water which water which prevents hot steam from entering the pressure gauge and keeps the gauge cool.Closed end of bourdon tube us attached to a toothed quadrant with help of link and pin.This quadrant meshes with a small pinion in the central spindle.

Working :When pressure is applied to inside to inside of oval boudon tube.It cross section tends to becomes circular,and free end of bourdon tube try to becomes straight,so turning the spindle by the links and gearing.This causes the needle to move and indicate pressure on graduated dial.

(2) Water level indicator

Function :It indicates the water level inside the boiler vessel.It shows level of water in the boiler drum.

Construction :Normally two water level indicators are fitted to the boiler.These are fitted at the front end of every boiler.Water level indicator consists of three cocks as steam cock,water cock and drain cock and glass tube with steam space.While water cock connects or disconnects the glass tube with water in the boiler.The drain cock is used to drain out the water from glass tube at intervals to ensures that the steam and water cocks are clear in operation.The glass tube is protected by means of a cover,and made of specially toghered glass.

Working :When steam cock and water cock opened,stean rushes from upper passage and the boiler.Two balls are places at the junction of metal tube.Under normal operating condition the balls are kept as shown in Fig in full line circle.In case the glass tube is broken steam will rushes from upper passage and water from lower passage due to pressure differece the ends of glass tube and then closed passages.This position of ball shown in Fig by dotted circle.Thus flow of water and steam out of boiler os prevented.

(3) Steam stop valve

Function :It regulates the flow of steam from boiler to the steam pipe or from one steam pipe to the other.

Construction :The common type of steam stop valve is shown in Fig.The flange of valve body bolted to the boiler at the highest part of steam space.It consist of valve seat and nut.The main body of valve is made of cast iron and valvle seat made from gun metal.

When steam stop valve is placed directly over the boiler and connected to the steam pipe line is called the Junction valve.If it is placed near to prime mover than normally called steam stop valve.

The lower ends of spindle connected to valve and upper end pass through gland and yoke,and connected with hand wheel.The gland is used to prevent the leakage of steam.

Working :The spindle is rotated by help of a hand wheel.Due to rotation of spindle the valve move up and down.When the valve sits over the valve seat,the passage of steam is vomplerely closed.The steam passage may be partially or fully opeaned by moving the valve up,help of rotation the hand wheel.The clearance(passage) between valve and valve seat regulates the flow of steam out of boiler.In locomotive boilers,the supply of steam is tegulatted by means of a regulator which is placed inside boiler shekk and operated by a handle from driver's cabin.
