History :

Mortal Kombat is a fighting video game series created by Midway Games. The first game in the series was released in 1992, and it quickly became popular for its unique characters, intense action, and over-the-top violence. The game's success spawned numerous sequels and spin-offs, and the series has become a cultural phenomenon.


The original Mortal Kombat game was developed as a response to the popular 1991 game Street Fighter II. It featured a roster of unique characters, each with their own special moves and abilities.



Description :

The game's violence, which included fatalities (finishing moves that killed the opponent) and blood, set it apart from other games at the time and sparked controversy. The game's success spawned numerous sequels, including Mortal Kombat II, Mortal Kombat 3, and Mortal Kombat: Deadly Alliance. These games expanded on the original game's formula and added new characters, game modes, and features.


In addition to the main series of games, the Mortal Kombat franchise has also spawned several spin-off games, including Mortal Kombat: Shaolin Monks, Mortal Kombat vs. DC Universe, and Mortal Kombat X.


Cinematic studio :

The franchise has also been adapted into a series of films, comics, and television shows.Today, Mortal Kombat remains a popular and enduring gaming franchise, with a dedicated fan base and regular releases of new games and content.

WB

Mortal Kombat is a fighting video game franchise developed and published by Warner Bros. Interactive Entertainment, a division of Warner Bros. Entertainment.

The franchise has a long history dating back to the original Mortal Kombat game, which was released in 1992. Over the years, the franchise has grown to include numerous sequels, spin-offs, and merchandise.

Warner Bros. has played a central role in the development and distribution of the Mortal Kombat franchise. The company has published all of the main games in the series, as well as various spin-offs and adaptations, including films, television shows, and comic books.

In addition, Warner Bros. has also been involved in the development and promotion of the franchise, working closely with the game developers to bring the Mortal Kombat universe to life.
